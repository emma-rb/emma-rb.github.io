About Me

The story behind the work

During an undergraduate internship at Unbound Now — a Bryan-College Station organization fighting human trafficking — I discovered what the nonprofit sector does best: meet people where they are, with creativity and care that larger institutions rarely afford. My supervisor there saw something in me and encouraged me to apply to Texas A&M's Bush School of Government and Public Service. I'm glad I listened.

Over two years at the Bush School I've written grants, stewarded donors, built funding pipelines, served as President of the Texas A&M chapter of the Association of Fundraising Professionals, and completed an IRB-approved capstone on nonprofit service delivery. I graduated in May 2026 with a 4.0 GPA. As I focused on fundraising, I realized that what I love most about nonprofit development is that relationships are at the center of every fundraising interaction. When you find someone who cares about a cause, fundraisers get to be the bridge that connects their generosity with work that matters.

I'm drawn to organizations that see fundraising as an invitation — welcoming people into a community of shared purpose rather than extracting transactions. And I'm increasingly focused on a gap I see across the sector: nonprofits doing vital, irreplaceable work, but running on tools and systems that can't keep up with their ambition. I want to help change that — one grant strategy, donor relationship, and workflow at a time.

Outside of work, you can find me in the garden, at the dog park, or planning my next trip. My travel bucket list includes scuba diving at the Great Barrier Reef and whale watching in the Pacific Northwest.

What I Stand For

01
Connecting People to Causes
I believe everyone has something they care about — and it's a fundraiser's privilege to make that match. That's the heart of why I do this work.
02
Relationships Before Transactions
Fundraising is an invitation into a community of shared purpose. I center long-term trust over short-term asks in every donor interaction.
03
Data with Purpose
Numbers tell us what's working. Stories tell us why it matters. I use both — rigorously and with care.
04
Tech as a Tool for Mission
Nonprofits often lag behind the tools available to them — not from lack of will, but lack of access. I'm passionate about closing that gap, from AI-assisted communications to smarter CRM and data practices for development work.
